Quantitative Signal Research for BTCUSDT Perpetual Futures

A research-only signal programme for Bitcoin perpetual futures on Binance. Thirteen candidate signals were tested; two survived a multi-stage kill discipline and are currently running in live paper trading.

Overview

Systematic signal research for BTC perpetual futures using a strict kill discipline and net-of-cost evaluation.

Universe

13 candidate signals tested across 2023 in-sample and five walk-forward out-of-sample windows from 2024 to 2026 YTD.

Outcome

2 signals survived. 11 were killed for cost failure, OOS failure, lack of independence, or insufficient data.

Methodology

Signals are screened against a cost-adjusted breakeven threshold derived from round-trip maker fees. Statistical significance alone is not enough; a signal must clear economic viability after costs and funding.

Item Detail
Evaluation framework Cost-adjusted breakeven hurdle with block bootstrap significance testing.
Bootstrap $B = 2{,}000$ with 21-day blocks to correct for serial autocorrelation.
Cost model Maker round-trip = 6 bp. Results are net of costs and include perpetual funding.
Walk-forward windows 2024 H1, 2024 H2, 2025 H1, 2025 H2, 2026 YTD.

Signal 1 - Validated

Fear-mean-reversion regime

199 trades · 2024-2026 OOS

General hypothesis: an options-market fear indicator predicts positive 24-hour returns when elevated above its recent baseline. The mechanism is mean reversion: fear peaks are followed by resolution. The signal is filtered out in low-fear environments where it shows no predictive power.

Limitations

  • Edge fades in sustained low-volatility regimes; filters reduce but do not eliminate inactivity.
  • Each 6-month OOS window has roughly 180 daily observations; forward evidence will sharpen conclusions.
  • Maker fills are assumed; volatile periods may drift toward taker costs.
  • Single-asset study on BTCUSDT Binance perpetuals; cross-asset generalisability is untested.
  • Paper trading only. No live capital at risk.
